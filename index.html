AI Powered Document Reader for Low-Vision Users

Main Mission: To make documents accessible Low-Vision Users.

Goals/Outcomes

Create a better document/image reader for low vision individuals. Users can drop digital documents or images into the web app, which will vocalize both the text and visual aspects/layout of the document.

Help make education more accessible.
Make different mediums more accessible.

Introduction

The goal of this project is to develop a web application that converts PDFs, handwritten text, and images into speech for Blind-Low Vision (BLV) users.

Background Information

The problem is critical because it aims to help the BLV community keep up with fast-changing technology, which has been heavily incorporated into education and advancements. Since enhancing visual consumption of information can be complicated and expensive, our project provides sound-based consumption as a complementary alternative.

Another issue is that much of the existing technology (such as AR glasses) is extremely costly. Our goal is to develop more affordable or open-source alternatives.

Ethical Sweep

General Questions

Should we even be doing this?
Yes, improving accessibility for BLV users is a meaningful application of AI, but we must be mindful of ethical concerns.

What might be the accuracy of a simple non-ML alternative?
A rule-based OCR system (software that turns printed or typed text from images into digital text using fixed rules) could achieve some success but would likely struggle with handwritten text and complex layouts.

What processes will we use to handle appeals/mistakes?
We will set up a process to review errors and adjust the training data or model parameters to improve accuracy over time.

How diverse is our team?
It’s pretty diverse!

Data Questions

Is our data valid for its intended use?
We need to verify that our training data includes diverse handwriting styles, fonts, and document structures.

What bias could be in our data?
Potential biases include overrepresentation of certain text styles or languages, which could lead to disparities in accuracy.

How could we minimize bias in our data and model?
We should collect diverse datasets and test performance across different user demographics.

How should we “audit” our code and data?
Regular audits should check for performance discrepancies across different groups and validate fairness in model outputs.

Impact Questions

Do we expect different error rates for different sub-groups in the data?
Yes, varying text styles and writing systems may lead to disparities that require targeted improvements.

What are likely misinterpretations of the results?
Users may over-rely on automated outputs, so we should provide disclaimers and encourage manual review where necessary.

How might we impinge individuals’ privacy and/or anonymity?
Ensuring that our tool does not store sensitive user data and follows strict privacy policies is crucial.
